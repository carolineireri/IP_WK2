The interesting naming tradition of the ghanian people.


In ghana, the Akan people mostly name their children after the day of the week they were born and the order in which they were born. These "day names" have further meanings concerning the soul and character of the person. Middle names have considerably more variety and can refer to their birth order, twin status, or an ancestor's middle name. This is a naming tradition that is shared throughout West Africa.
